üîí Cyber Security Overview

Cyber Security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks.

It involves measures to prevent unauthorized access, data breaches, and other cyber threats.

Advanced cyber security includes encryption, intrusion detection, firewalls, and monitoring.

Regular security audits and risk assessments are crucial.

Employee training and awareness are key to preventing social engineering attacks.

Strong authentication protocols enhance system protection.

Cloud security strategies protect data stored remotely.

Endpoint security is essential for devices connecting to the network.

Incident response plans ensure rapid action in case of breaches.

Continuous research and updates keep security measures current and effective.

üö® Cyber Crime

Cybercrime is criminal activity that uses computers, networks, or digital devices to commit illegal acts like fraud, identity theft, and malware attacks. These crimes, which can be financially motivated or aimed at causing damage, exploit digital vulnerabilities and can have far-reaching consequences for individuals, businesses, and governments.

Hackers: A hacker is a person with expertise in information technology who gains unauthorized access to a computer system, often by exploiting vulnerabilities for criminal purposes like stealing data or money.

Worms: Computer worms are a type of self-replicating malware that can spread across networks to cause harm, including system disruption, data theft, and creating backdoors for remote access. They exploit software vulnerabilities or spread through email attachments, infected websites, or file-sharing networks without user intervention.

Viruses: Viruses are malicious programs designed to steal data, cause damage, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems for financial gain, espionage, or disruption.
